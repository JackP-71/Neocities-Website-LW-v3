One Eye, One Girl

Currently reading: Chapter 1

You are currently reading chapter 1, nothing has happened so far... but this will be used for other chapters :)

I wouldn’t say I’m the biggest fan of school, especially as it is the main source of all the bullying. So, it’s a bit of a shame that I’m here nearly all the time. Hazelwood School was where I found myself at. It was the second school I had been to this year and, just 5 months in, it seems most people have turned against me. Why? You may be asking, well, I was born without my right eye; it was because of this minor defect, that I was bullied.

Discrimination is truly terrible sometimes.

It’s been just five months, which may seem like a long time. However, it was just the right amount of time for me to scope out who was going to be the most annoying.

Walking into the maths classroom on a dreary Monday morning. Dreary in the sense that the sky is covered in dark clouds and I suppose it’s also because I’m not the biggest fan of maths. Anyway, walking into the classroom, I see the most irritating being in the entire school. Evie. The red-haired bully, as I call her. I think red has a connotation of being an evil colour, so I suppose it makes sense that the one red haired girl in the year would be my enemy.

She’s talking with two of her friends, who I suppose are also my enemies. I’m not too sure on their names, but I have a feeling they are called Phoebe and Maisie. Phoebe is the one with light hair and Maisie has the darker brown hair. Now that I know who the bullies are, I know to steer clear from them whenever I see them in the corridors. It’s because of people like this that I have taught myself that it is very difficult to blend it, especially as it is nearly completely impossible to do that when you were born to stand out.

It wasn’t like I tried to do that anyway, I wear an eye patch, which was made to be a similar shade to my skin, over where my eye should be, but even that doesn’t seem to work.

There are a few other girls in my class, but unlike my last school, I had actually managed to make friends with two of them. Beatrice, it was always Beatrice and never Bea (she hates it, but never told me why), is the one I see sat at a table on the front row, not too sure why she did that, because neither of us like maths that much. She has light blonde hair, when I sit down at an empty desk next to her, she looks over at me with her eyes, which interestingly were blue and brown, I don’t think I had seen anyone with heterochromia before, although if she didn’t get bullied for being different, why did I? “Hello, Iris” she says.

I simply say “hi” back, because I’m still a bit tired. Beatrice seems to have a few other friends other than me and Willow, my other friend, who isn’t here yet. Although she spends most of her time with me and her.

The door opens, I was expecting it to be the teacher, but instead, Willow arrived, looked around the classroom, and kept her head down as she walked over to the tables where we were sat. She sits down at the desk on the other side of Beatrice. Willow has dark brown hair, which is so long it’s close to covering her dark blue eyes. She didn’t greet any of us, but we were in quite a noisy classroom, I was guessing it was the bullies making all the noise, although, she does stay quiet most of the time, especially when we are in lessons. She does speak a bit more when it’s break or lunch time when we are sitting on the field.

Now we were just waiting for the teacher to arrive. I decide look around the classroom, which are all basically the same no matter what lesson you are in. Three or so rows of chairs and tables, the teacher’s desk and a chalkboard. There are a few displays of students work pinned on a few bulletin boards along the pale walls.

Eventually, our maths teacher: Mr Hughes walks into the classroom holding a briefcase and apologises for being late. I hear a bit of shuffling around behind me, as everyone sits down. I can only really describe him as a human calculator, which is something I don’t think I’ll ever be. A problem with this is that he doesn’t do the greatest job of explaining things. I think it’s because he knows it so just assumes that we all know it to, which, most of the time we don’t. So, even if we are learning something new, there may as well be no point.

The maths lesson goes by fairly quickly and my brain can be put at rest as it is break time next. I decide to break away from my friends and take the slightly longer way out to the field. I wanted to be alone for a bit.

The school corridors are usually a bit busy as everyone heads to the main hall, so I have to push past a few students to get there. The main hall was the main location for recreational times in the timetable and assemblies. The main hall can get a bit busy though, so I usually go out onto the field; right now, though, I just wanted to be alone. When I arrive at the main hall, there are quite a lot of students there and a few teachers there to ensure no trouble is caused.

Thankfully, most people were sat down, so I didn’t have to push past many people to get to the door leading out to the field like you sometimes had to do.

Arriving at the back of the main hall, I push the door open and am greeted with the fresh outdoor air. At least that helps me to forget about the six different lessons I have to do throughout the day.

Closing the door behind me, I notice my friends were already at the back of the field, sat by the dark green fence. It acts as the perimeter between Hazelwood and the outside world and also separates the field from the school garden. It’s been a while since I last checked out the garden, so I begin to walk alongside it.

It was all going peacefully, until I see a shadow creeping behind me. I don’t really want to look back; in case a problem is caused. But I can’t resist the temptation, so I look back and see three figures I don’t want to see. Evie and her two friends were following me...

“Why are you following me?” I ask, feeling a bit annoyed. I just wanted today to be one of those rare days where I wasn’t bullied, especially for the first day in the week.

“Speaking to me?” Evie avoids the question.

“Yes, because who else could I be talking to?” I say, especially as they were the only three people near me.

“I don’t know, yourself?” The red-haired bully comments in a quieter voice, (probably thinking I wouldn’t hear). This was getting ridiculous, after a few moments of silence. she says “Well, in that case... No, I wasn’t following you, I wanted to... to observe the scenery”. She was clearly trying to sound innocent, but I could see right past the lie.

I suppose she could have been looking at the gardens; though I had a feeling she wouldn’t be.

“Oh, alright then.” I simply say, attempting to avoid an argument. I only wanted to be alone this one time! I look down at the green grass and see Evie’s shadow speed up. I decide to do the same, so it isn’t long before we are both sprinting down the field.

Being part of the cross-country team does help a bit with staying away from Evie, Phoebe and Maisie, but as we turn the corner, going past my friends, my breath begins to run out and I slow down slightly. A mistake which cannot be helped. I look back and Evie was catching up, she was also part of the cross-country team, so I wasn’t too surprised, maybe this was where the bullying was stemming from. I continue running for a few moments longer until I trip over my legs and fall onto the jade green grass, made brighter from the summer’s sun.

Once I had fallen, I knew that was it. Game over. It was definitely the case when all three bullies began to surround me. There was no escaping it now.

In a slight panic, I wonder what the best way to escape this is. As I’m thinking of something, Evie kneels down and attempts to rip my eye patch off.

“What are you doing?!” I exclaim, trying to get her hands off my eye patch.

She doesn’t reply and as much as I try to pry her hands off my eye patch, it doesn’t seem to work. Then, inspiration strikes. So, I decide to put my plan into action.

The plan was simple. All I did, was kick Evie in the legs.

She stumbles back and says “Ugh, what did you do that for? You freak!” she stands up and dusts, what appears to be nothing, off her legs.

She then stands back where she was before. That plan had failed.

She didn’t give me an answer the first time, so, she certainly wouldn’t be getting one from me. Besides, I couldn’t answer, because my mind was already fixated on looking for the next way to escape.

Looking up, noticing the bullies surrounding me, but they aren’t the only people I see, I also see a small crowd beginning to form. I wonder whether her calling me a freak had something to do with it. I was guessing this wouldn’t be the last time this would happen. I continued to think of a plan, but was running out of ideas now.

And, then I had it. I shuffle away from the crowd of people, until I could feel my hair touch someone’s legs. I then begin to stand up. I realised a flaw in my plan when Maisie stuck her leg out, tripping me over. Bringing me back to the start of my plan.

Whilst I was thinking about the next part of my plan, I was looking down at the grass. Refusing to make eye contact with the school bullies, I notice something. On the blades of grass, there was a scattering of a metallic red liquid, which I was sure wasn’t there before. I then put my finger up to my nose and pulled them away to reveal the fact that they were covered in a scarlet red colour. That could only mean one thing: my nose was bleeding (which wasn’t an uncommon occurrence for me).

I wasn’t going to give up. Not now. Not after all the bullying I had been through. My plan was somewhat successful this time. I managed to stand up again, avoiding the bully’s attempt to trip me up.

After this part of my plan was successful, I saw an opening in the crowd and began to make a run for it. I didn’t care if this wouldn’t last long. I didn’t care that my nose was bleeding. I just wanted to be away from all of this.

And, the free feeling didn’t last long. It lasted for about ten seconds, before Evie caught up to me, whether or not she was a faster runner than me or I was running slower than usual, I don’t know. Just like Maisie before, she stuck her leg out and down I went again.

It would normally be at this point where I’d have given up. But I was so tired of being constantly bullied because of my missing right eye, I decided that I didn’t want to be defeated by the school bullies, especially on a Monday (which typically sets the tone for the rest of the week in my opinion).

So, I began to formulate, what would hopefully be, the final plan for escaping this. All the while, I could hear some people in the crowd, mainly the bullies, saying stuff like “fight!” and “You’re a disgrace, Iris” and I think I also heard someone say “Oh, it’s just that one eyed gremlin.” I was quite tempted to punch the person who said that last one, especially if it was one of the three bullies.

Thankfully, the a plan had occurred to me and now all I had to do was put it into action. Which I was about to do. But, then all of a sudden, everything fell silent as a loud voice echoed across the field.

“Everyone stop!” A voice, which I recognised as my geography teacher’s, yelled. She then asked “What on earth is going on here? What do you all think you are doing?” I looked around at the faces of all the school bullies, they all seemed defeated now. They all refused to make eye contact with the teacher, who told them to go to the had master’s office, all three of them obeyed her only command as they all began to traipse in the direction of the school.

When they began to move away, I was met face to face with my geography teacher. At the sight of me, her face turned deathly pale as she says “Iris, you need to go and clear yourself up...” and then she adds “And then you should probably also head to the head master’s office.”

I stand back up as the crowd began to disperse and stumbled my way back to the school building, after all, all that running had made my legs very tired. I look back, all that remained was a trail of blood following me on my journey to the school toilets.
