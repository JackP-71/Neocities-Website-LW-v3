One Eye, One Girl

Currently reading: Chapter 2

Previous Happenings:

After a fight with Evie, Iris heads back to the school building, dreading a visit to the headmaster's office.

When I walked into the school building, Beatrice joined by my side and asked "What happened out there?"

“Well... You can probably guess.” I say, looking forwards and seeing the group of school bullies turning the corner as they continue their way to the head master’s office. As much as I hoped that would be the last of them, I had to face facts, it wouldn’t be.

Besides, I remembered that I would also have to head down that corridor in a few minutes.

“Yeah, I could tell before as well.” I wasn’t too surprised by this because I had a feeling everyone saw what happened. “We were the ones to get the teacher” I was assuming ‘we’ meant her and Willow, being sat at the corner of the field, they probably saw the whole situation unfold.

“I suppose that makes sense.”

“I suppose anyone could guess something was up though, because you don’t look in a good way right now.” My friend tells me as I arrive at the toilets. I had no idea what Beatrice meant by “You don’t look in a good way,” however, when I came face to face with my reflection, it all became clear (well... It did when I swept my arm across the mirror to see, they clearly didn’t pay much attention to the mirrors when cleaning). The bottom half of my face is almost entirely covered in my own blood and my nose is still bleeding.

So, I grab a tissue and hold it by my nose to ease the nose bleed. I decide to take a peek outside of the lavatories and found a trail of blood on the floor. Every single red droplet indicating every step I had taken.

It took a little while before my nose bleed came to a stop. Everyone had returned to lessons now, so I wasn’t expecting to hear the sound of someone's footsteps getting louder and louder. I cautiously take a step out into the main hall and see Willow traipsing towards me. When she arrives at the door frame, she gives a little sigh before saying “I didn’t think you’d still be here.”

“Unfortunately, I am." I remark "But I think my nose bleed has stopped now. Give me a few minutes.”

“Wait... don’t you have to go to the head master’s office?”

"Yeah... I guess I do," It was the very thing i was thinking of before Willow showed up to the main hall, As much as I was secretly hoping the head master would have forgotten about it, but since he was probably already talking to the school bullies, he wouldn't have. In fact, it was supposedly common knowledge at Hazelwood that the head master never seemed to forget anything, which did seem slightly eerie.

Willow wishes me good luck for when I have to head to the teacher’s office. Whilst she goes to the toilet, I turn back to my reflection.

My solitary green eye stares back at me and I realise my eye patch was folded slightly, its primary purpose was covering the empty socket where my other eye was supposed to go. My parents made me get one, because they realised that the other kids would think it would look weird, and I don’t blame them, but this in itself is the cause of the bullying at the minute. After correcting my eye patch, I look around and then take it off. why can’t people just accept me for who I am?

I go into a haze for a while before snapping back to reality when Willow returns and washes her hands. As Willow says goodbye again, I quickly place my eye patch back over my eye and then go about clearing some of the blood from my face. In doing this, I also realise that some had even managed to make its way into my light brown hair, I don’t even know how. But I clear that up as well.

Once I had removed all traces of blood from my pale orange blazer and my hair. I double check to see if there is any more blood, but there isn’t. I then leave the toilets and begin my journey down the corridor to the head master’s office to meet my fate.

Maybe that was a slight over exaggeration. But I may as well be meeting my fate, the corridor was eerily quiet. This was peculiar because there were classrooms positioned alongside the corridor, but, even though you could see the lessons taking place within them, you couldn’t hear anything...

For nearly all of the classrooms, you are able to hear the lessons taking place inside. These four rooms (two on either side) were very quiet. It was almost as if they were built differently to the other classrooms. Just to build up the suspense. Who knows, maybe if the doors were open, you may still not be able to hear anything, with that being said, I don’t think I’ve ever seen these doors being open during my few visits to the head master’s office.

The rather unusual foreboding sense didn't stop there. Because, when you arrive at the end of the corridor, there is just one door leading to the office. In case you were wondering, it was just a regular dark wooden door with a small window to the left side and a handle on the right (not too dissimilar from the other doors you see around Hazelwood). In fact, the only difference to note on the door was a small wooden plaque which had the head master’s name: Mr. Thorpe printed on it and then also ‘head master of Hazelwood School’ written underneath it.

I took a seat on one of the two benches on either side of the corridor’s walls. It was also a little weird that you were able to hear what is going on inside the office. Maybe the head master talks loudly? I’m unsure... Every time I’m in the office he doesn’t seem to speak that loudly. Especially considering the silence of the other rooms.

As I sit there for a steadily increasing amount of time, I begin to feel nervous. Even though I had done this about a million times before. Although... The plan I carried out would probably put me into trouble as well. Which was annoying, because I wouldn’t have had to have done that if the bullies didn’t start what they did.

Maybe this nervous feeling wouldn’t be too bad if I thought of a few good things...

Unfortunately, and rather irritatingly, I could only think of a few bad things at the moment:

  • Being bullied - why can't all of this just be put to an end?
  • School - I mean, it's where all the bullies are. I've had to move school two times because the bullying still follows me!
  • This hallway... in a way - After all, I've had to walk down it quite a few times now and it does get a little repetitive after a while.

Maybe some good things will enter my mind later...

It takes a few more minutes of muffled conversation before I hear the head master’s voice say “You are all dismissed!”

The door then opens and the three bullies walk out. As they walk past me, I can hear they are having a conversation but don’t pay much attention to what they are saying. However, my attention turns when Evie turns to face me before she turns back and, walking away, she says “He didn’t really punish us. Surely, that freak is going to be punished!” As soon as she had said that, I was very tempted to shout back “I am not a freak!” but I ultimately resisted them temptation as I wondered had they even been given a punishment at all? They were the ones who started it after all.

I would like to assume that all three of them got punished after all. Before I had any more time to ponder upon this, the head master's voice echoed through the walls "Enter!" This is it... again.

I stood up from where I was sitting, I’ve always found it strange how the head master could just detect someone’s present, maybe he saw me traipsing down the corridor? I don’t really know how he knew I was there. Maybe he saw my shadow? I guess it could be something to add to the list of Hazelwood mysteries (of which there are a few more than I thought there would be). I then took a deep breath. Opened the wooden door, and then took a step inside.

Having been here a number of times before, nothing seemed new anymore. The pale-yellow wallpaper was still fading away ever so slightly, maybe more so since I was last here. The dark wooden accents surrounding the door and the windows, the glossy dark wood surface of the desk, the laptop and the mug filled with pencils on the desk. Nothing had changed. The second thing which may have changed was the amount of dust covering a picture frame which was positioned to the left side of the head master’s desk, when I had arrived at Hazelwood, I had to visit the office for an introduction and the picture frame was still covered in quite a bit of dust. So, I wasn’t able to see who the person in the picture frame was... maybe it’s another thing to add to the list of Hazelwood mysteries.

I put my hand up to the door to prevent it from slamming shut, and then walk over to the dark red rug, positioned towards the centre of the room. It covers part of the lighter shade wood of the floor.

With nowhere apparent to sit down, I remain stood up as the head master gets straight to the point as to why I was bought here.

“The others have told me about what happened during break time. So, now I have their side of the story, I want you to share yours. I need you to tell me everything that took place, Iris.” I give a little sigh before beginning my retelling of the event. Everything from Evie following me on the field to the fight which then broke out and my geography teacher putting an end to it. All the while I am saying this, he nods his head a few times and writes some things down on a piece of paper.

Once I finish telling him everything (or what I could remember), he finishes writing his notes and picks up two pieces of paper, both with writing on them. He must have made the bullies do this as well. Once he finishes comparing the two pieces of paper, he looks up and tells me “What you have told me may be true, but it doesn’t align with what the other three have told me, they tell me you started it. Is this true?”

I wasn’t entirely surprised by this because of course they would have twisted the tale to pin the blame on me. At that moment, I felt the anger rising in me, so I just decide to release the anger from the cage which I have tried to contain it in for the past three or so months I have been at Hazelwood for.

“No... why would I start it?! They are the ones who have been bullying me for the past three months, and of course the blame is always placed on me!” Which was sort of true, I mean, every time I seem to be brought here after some sort of bullying, I’m always the one who gets blamed for it, and I can never seem to change that. I then mutter under my breath, “How on earth are they still here” Because I’ve seen people get removed from schools for this kind of thing before.

It actually felt quite good to say all those thoughts aloud, or at least some of them.

Instead, the head master continues by saying “Ok, let’s try not to get angry about this, because you were also part of the problem.” The few minutes of silence that followed must have made me seem confused... even though I had a feeling I knew where this was going. “You fighting back makes the situation worse, and if I’m right in thinking, this is the second or third time you’ve been here in the past two weeks?” In nod my head, it seems right, but I’ve lost track now. “Anyway, if this continues Iris, we may have no choice but to send people like you elsewhere” Please tell me he told the bullies that as well. What does he mean ‘like me’? I’m the target of all the bullying, why would they expel me and not the bullies.

“Wait... w... what?!” I stutter. How can the bullies evade the punishment and it always end up on me?

“As you know bullying, especially fighting, is not tolerated in this school. Fighting back will only make the situation worse for everyone involved.” The head master continues with this conversation but my attention drifts elsewhere. I’m so bored of being in the room now. Eventually, the head master falls silent and I have a feeling he’s waiting for me to say something.

What could I say? My mind could barely think of anything to say... I was just speechless at how this conversation is going. Eventually, I end up saying “How... why will I have to be expelled? I’ve barely done anything, because of one small thing, people bully me! What am I supposed to do about that?”

“I understand that this is annoying, but I am going to have to punish you all the same.”

Fighting back now wasn’t going to do anything. Evie had said she and her friends had barely been punished and I was still wondering what that meant. I sincerely hoped she, and I guess her two friends, would be punished, after all, fighting is wrong, in the words of the head master.

When the head master asked if I had any other questions regarding this, I asked what my punishment was and he told me that I would be forced to miss break times for a week, which wasn’t even a bad punishment in my eye. After all, it would mean I avoid seeing the bullies. Although, with that out of the way, there was one final thing I had to know “If I’m getting punished, then is Evie and the rest of them getting punished as well?”

“I needed your input on the situation before I gave them a punishment. But, now that I have your input on the situation, I may consider a greater punishment for the other three involved now that I have everyone’s view on the event.” After a short while of silence, I assume the meeting has ended, so I walk over to the door and as I’m about to open it, the head master says “Iris, I’m sure you’re bored of coming here most of the time, so could you try to not get into any other fights for the rest of the year?”

I look back but look down at the floor to avoid making eye contact with him before whispering under my breath “That’s going to be impossible...” The head master asked what I had said, he must have heard my quiet remark, I quickly say “Nothing” and leave the room.

Shutting the door behind me, I begin thinking that it wasn’t too bad, then I remember how I’m not supposed to get into any fights for the rest of the year, I dismissed this as being quite a tough challenge, because it wasn’t even me who started any of the fights or bullying(although, at least the punishment wasn't too bad). It was Evie and her two friends, who didn’t really seem like the sort of friends you would want to me.

As I’m wondering about all that, I walk into the main hall and check the clock and find that there is about twenty-five minutes left of history, so I decide to head to the classroom.

Arriving at the classroom, I open the door to find that the class seemed to be co-operating in some group activity. I scan the classroom and find my friends sat at a table near the teacher’s desk, so I walk over to that table.

When I sit down, I ask “What’s going on?” Because we never seem to do anything like this, especially in history. “What are we doing?”

“We’re doing a project,” Beatrice explains, “We’re researching the history of different things; we had to give a topic to the teacher and that’s what we’re working on now.” which I suppose was explained the A3 sheets of paper on everyone’s desks. But we never seemed to do this sort of thing in any of the lessons, with the exception of art (but especially not history). Usually, we would be writing notes on practically the teacher was saying, like in most lessons. Today seemed to be different.

“What are we researching for this?”

“Truth be told, I wasn’t sure... Willow didn’t give many suggestions and I wasn’t too sure myself, so I quickly thought of something along the lines of discovering the history of people.”

“So, it’s basically Biology?” I ask, because that’s what came to mind first.

“I suppose so... obviously, we have to focus more on the history part of it,”

“I guess that's obvious,"

Beatrice begins writing on the A3 piece of paper again before asking “Oh yeah... how did your trip to the headmaster’s office go?”

I think about how the punishment wasn’t too bad and then how he told me how I should try to not get into any more fights this year. “It could have gone worse, I suppose. I’ll tell you more after the lesson is over” Which when I checked the time would only be ten minutes away.
